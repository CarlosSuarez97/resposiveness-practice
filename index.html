My Top Two Albums

My current top two albums in 2024

eternal shunshine

"eternal sunshine" is the seventh studio album by American singer-songwriter Ariana Grande, released on March 8th, 2024. It is her first full-length studio album in almost four years, following the release of "Positions" in 2020. It was preceeded by the lead single "yes, and?", which debuted atop the Billboard Hot 100, earning Grande her eigth number one song in the US, and her sixth one to debut atop of the chart.

The album was further supported by the singles "we can't be friends (wait for your love)" and "the boy is mine", with the former also debuting atop of the Billboard Hot 100, making Grande the female artis with the most number one debuts in history with seven.

A "slightly deluxe" edition of the album was released digitally to streaming services, and Grande's website, two days later, featuring four remixes and guest appereances from American singer Mariah Carey and Australian singer Troye Sivan.

Red (Taylor's Version)

"Red (Taylor's Version)" is an album released by American singer-songwriter Taylor Swift in 2021. It is a re-recording of her fourth studio album, "Red", originally released in 2012.

The album is part of Swift's re-recording projects following the 2019 dispute over the ownership of her back catalog.

Released seven months after "Fearless (Taylor's Version)", the re-recording of her second studio album "Fearless", "Red (Taylor's Version)" features a total of 30 songs. In addition to the songs present on both the standard and deluxe editions of the original version of the album, it also features a re-recorded version of the charity single "Ronan", a ten minute version of the song "All Too Well" (which went on to become the longest song to top the Bilboard Hot 100), solo versions of the songs "Babe" and "Better Man" (tracks Swift wrote and were then recorded by Sugarland and Little Big Town respectively), and six that'd been cut from the original track list, dubbed as "From The Vault".

The album also features guest appereances from English singer Ed Sheeran and American singers Phoebe Bridgers and Chris Stapleton.
